Soloutions and UN Goals

Target 15.6

Adopting legislative, administrative and policy frameworks

By the end of 2022, the legislative, administrative and policy measures had increased through the implementation of laws by 68 countries worldwide in relation to the Nogoya Protocol Protocol. Alongside this, 88 countries put in place measures for the implementation of the International Treaty on Plant Genetic Resources for Food and Agriculture.

Restoration of degraded land


The land that is either no longer needed or has become degraded needs to be restored. So that the original ecosystem, biodiversity, and natural cycles can go back to as they once were before. By doing so we can restore some of the land that we took over for purposes like agriculture or mining.


Learn More

Stop expanding agriculture footprint

Stop the expansion of agricultural land, like the ones that are eating up tropical forests and savannas. By doing this we can reduce the amount of forests we cut down, which also reduces carbon emissions.

Target 15.9

Integrate ecosystem and biodiversity values into national and local planning

In regards to biodiversity, there have been an increase in the number of countries incorporating these values into their accounting and reporting systems. Including the 92 countries that have indicated implementation of the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA) as of 2023.
