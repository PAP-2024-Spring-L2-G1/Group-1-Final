What do we use out land for?

Around 44% of the world’s habitable land goes to farm land, which is about 5 times the size of the U.S. But only 55 % of the food grown go towards feeding people, while 36% of it go towards feeding livestock, and the remaining amount go towards bio fuel, and other industrial usages.

The impacts of taking over these land, and remaking them into farmland has caused:

  • Diminished biodiversity

  • Land Degeneration

  • Changes in Earth’s natural nitrogen and phosphorous cycles

These farm land are not only impacting the land that are being taken over to house them, but the rivers, lakes, and oceans around them. The main issue is that these farm lands are producing a large amount of pollution and fresh water usage, as farmers don’t properly utilize fertilizers, and water methods for their crops.
